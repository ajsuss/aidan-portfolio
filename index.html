About Me

I’m a passionate mechanical and mechatronics engineer with a deep enthusiasm for designing, controlling and applying robotic systems in the real world. My studies at UC Berkeley (B.S. Mechanical Engineering, minor in EECS, May 2024; M.S. Mechanical Engineering, May 2025) and hands‑on experiences at Tesla, UC Berkeley labs and competition teams have shaped a broad skillset including CAD (SolidWorks, CATIA, Fusion 360), FEA/FEM, control algorithms (PID, UKF, EKF, PF), embedded programming (C/C++, Python, ROS) and pneumatic/electro‑mechanical system integration.

Beyond formal coursework I’ve led and contributed to projects ranging from battlebots and flywheel‑stabilized bicycles to CNC plotters, autonomous rovers and signal‑processing algorithms. I thrive on rapid prototyping and iterative design, and I’m driven by the challenge of turning ambitious ideas into robust, manufacturable hardware.
