Thermal Expansion Experiment

Measuring linear and volumetric expansion coefficients of metals.

Goal

This experiment measured the linear and volumetric thermal‑expansion coefficients of 6061 aluminum, 110 brass and 464 copper using thermocouples and thermal imaging.

Method

Metal rods were submerged in temperature baths ranging from 0–100 °C. Temperatures were recorded with thermocouples and a FLIR camera, and length and diameter changes were measured with a micrometer.

Analysis

Plots of ΔL/L versus ΔT were generated and linear regression was performed to extract the linear coefficient α. The volumetric coefficient β was calculated from radial expansion, and uncertainties were estimated using sensor specifications.

Outcome

The measured coefficients were within approximately 10 % of handbook values. The experiment underscored the importance of emissivity control, rapid handling and instrument calibration to minimise systematic errors.
