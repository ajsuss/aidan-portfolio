Automated Pneumatic Tooling

Automated press design for Tesla assembly lines.

Overview

This project involved designing and building a fully automated press to install coolant‑pump mounting brackets on Tesla’s assembly line. The goal was to replace a manual choke point, improving ergonomics and reducing cycle time.

Mechanical Design Highlights

A custom fixture matched bracket and pump profiles for positive seating and was 3D‑printed in durable resin. The machine used an extrusion‑rail frame with a linear‑guide sliding tray for part loading and unloading, and a tailored pressing foot with an integrated indicator to verify proper seating.

Process & Pneumatic Logic

Operators load parts into the fixture and engage dual safety buttons. The tray retracts into a semi‑enclosed chamber where a pneumatic piston press‑fits the bracket. The air‑logic system uses 5/2 and 3/2 valves with regulated air at 60–80 psi; dual‑button inputs act as an AND gate to prevent accidental activation.

Safety, Ergonomics & Outcomes

Pinch‑point guards, acrylic panels, color‑coded labels and two‑hand operation ensure safe use. The design eliminated the previous bottleneck and reduced cycle time below takt. The footprint accommodates two fixtures in parallel and can scale to multiple units off a single pneumatic bank.
