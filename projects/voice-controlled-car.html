Voice‑Controlled Car

Battery‑powered miniature car that responds to spoken commands.

Overview

For the EECS 16B semester project, my team built an autonomous miniature car that responds to four spoken commands using on‑board machine learning and closed‑loop control.

Hardware Labs

We constructed analog filters, DAC/ADC interfaces and drive electronics. The car integrates dual DC motors with encoders, a microphone front‑end and an audio filter bank.

Control & Algorithms

Motor dynamics were modelled via system identification. We implemented open‑loop and feedback control for straight‑line driving and designed a PCA/SVD‑based voice classifier for spoken commands.

Integration & Demo

By combining the audio classifier, control algorithms and drive hardware, the car reliably executed commands: “drive far,” “drive close,” “turn left” and “turn right.”
