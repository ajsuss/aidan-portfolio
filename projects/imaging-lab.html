Imaging Lab

Single‑pixel imaging and reconstruction using linear algebra.

Objective

The goal of this EECS 16A lab was to build a single‑pixel imaging setup using a light sensor and projector to capture drawings and reconstruct them via linear‑algebra techniques.

Implementation

Python/NumPy scripts generated masking matrices, captured data and reconstructed images. The projector and sensor were aligned, and mask display was synchronised with sampling to maximise signal quality.

Results

The system successfully reconstructed grayscale and RGB images from single‑pixel scans. Hadamard multi‑pixel masks were developed for higher signal‑to‑noise ratios, enabling exploration of the trade‑offs between resolution and noise.

Lessons

This lab highlighted the importance of well‑conditioned measurement matrices and connected theoretical linear algebra concepts with practical imaging hardware.
