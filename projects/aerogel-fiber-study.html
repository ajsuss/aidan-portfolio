Aerogel Fiber Study

Evaluating spinning and drying techniques for flexible insulating textiles.

Overview

As part of a literature review, I compared aerogel fibre fabrication methods such as wet spinning, electrospinning, wet coaxial spinning and freeze spinning, along with drying techniques like freeze drying, supercritical drying and ambient pressure drying.

Contribution

I authored and refined sections on spinning techniques and drying method evaluations, compiled and formatted the reference list and integrated team feedback to enhance clarity and coherence.

Findings

The review synthesised data on process scalability, mechanical performance, pore architecture and thermal conductivity. A hybrid honeycomb‑reinforced laminate design was proposed to overcome aerogel fragility for large‑area textiles.

Skills

This project strengthened my technical writing, literature synthesis and collaboration skills and improved my ability to critically evaluate advanced materials processing techniques.
