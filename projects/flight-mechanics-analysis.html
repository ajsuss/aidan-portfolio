Flight Mechanics Analysis

Centre‑of‑gravity envelope analysis for an Embraer E195‑E2.

Objective

I calculated the forward and aft centre‑of‑gravity limits for an Embraer E195‑E2 across its operational weight range and Mach numbers to ensure trim and stability.

Method

CG limits were derived by varying tail incidence and wing‑body moment coefficients. Static stability and balance constraints were applied. MATLAB sweeps over CG and mass grids generated the envelope and visualised results.

Results

I identified the smallest CG range at Mach 0.74 and mapped the allowable envelope. Stability metrics such as pitching‑moment coefficient, pitch‑moment slope and angle of attack were explored. Tail‑volume adjustments were analysed to reduce high‑α regions.

Skills

This project applied flight‑mechanics theory, vectorised MATLAB computations and surface visualisations. It improved my workflow for analytical derivation, numerical validation and design optimisation.
