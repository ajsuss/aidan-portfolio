Exoplanet Habitability Research

Transit‑method analysis of exoplanet HAT‑P‑12b.

Background

In this ULab research project I analysed the dip in starlight when the exoplanet HAT‑P‑12b passes in front of its host star. This transit method allows estimation of orbital radius, period and planetary size.

Methodology

Using MicroObservatory, I selected the target exoplanet and collected 80–90 images during a transit event. I cleaned the brightness data in Python, produced light curves and used polynomial fits to identify the transit.

Analysis

I plotted relative brightness versus time, removed outliers and created binned light curves. Key characteristics such as planet radius, velocity and semi‑major axis were calculated. Comparison to Earth and the star’s habitable zone revealed that HAT‑P‑12b is significantly larger, faster and outside the habitable zone.

Future Work

Future research could involve spectroscopy to determine atmospheric composition, analysing a larger sample of exoplanets and incorporating habitability indices like the Earth Similarity Index.
