MATLAB Beam Analysis

Automated shear, moment and stress analysis for a beam under combined loads.

Goal

In this solid mechanics project, I automated the shear, moment and stress analysis of a simply supported beam subjected to a point load and a distributed load. The target was to size a circular cross‑section so that the maximum principal stress remained below 270 MPa.

Major Deliverables

I produced hand‑derived shear and bending moment diagrams for validation, along with four MATLAB scripts: vmd.m computes shear and moment diagrams; stress.m evaluates stresses for a given diameter; caseEqual.m implements a bisection algorithm for equal segment lengths; and caseGeneral.m sweeps segment lengths to generate a design‑diameter envelope.

Analytical & Computational Workflow

Support reactions were calculated via equilibrium. Closed‑form expressions for shear and moment were sampled at 100 points in MATLAB. Principal stress tensors were constructed, and the maximum stress was extracted via eigenvalues. A bisection algorithm sized the diameter so that the maximum stress equalled 270 MPa.

Plotting Results

Automated plots show the beam setup, shear and moment diagrams, and the minimum diameter and design envelope for various segment lengths. These tools enable engineers to size beams quickly for similar load cases.
