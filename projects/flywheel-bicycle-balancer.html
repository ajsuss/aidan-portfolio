Flywheel Bicycle Balancer

Gyroscopic module that lets a bicycle self‑correct lean at low speeds.

Project Overview

This mechatronics capstone project explored using gyroscopic forces to stabilise a bicycle at low speeds. We conceived an add‑on flywheel module that produces counter‑torque to re‑center the bike when it begins to lean.

Core Concept & Architecture

An inverted pendulum flywheel stores angular momentum. A high‑power brushless DC motor repositions the flywheel to deliver corrective torque. A magnetic encoder measures lean angle and an ESP32 microcontroller computes control commands sent through a VESC motor controller.

Key Design Decisions

We selected a Scorpion SII‑4035‑450 KV motor for its power density, designed a steel flywheel using a Lagrangian energy model to balance mass and moment of inertia, and chose a Maytech 100 A VESC paired with a high‑resolution magnetic encoder.

Embedded Control & Software

Firmware written in C++/Arduino on the ESP32 implemented state‑variable current control and a state machine governing startup, balancing and fault shutdown. Modular encoder and IMU drivers enable future upgrades.

Accomplishments & Lessons

The prototype demonstrated live low‑speed balance correction, counteracting gusts, uneven terrain and rider shifts. We fabricated, wired and programmed the system in six weeks. Future work includes addressing latency and control saturation in the VESC and exploring higher‑resolution sensors.
