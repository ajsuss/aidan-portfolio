Manufacturing Design Project

Slide‑out side table with formal engineering drawings and tolerancing.

Overview

This project involved designing and prototyping a slide‑out side table that clamps beneath a desk to expand workspace. The emphasis was on applying formal engineering drawings, manufacturing processes and geometric tolerancing to create a detailed prototype.

GD&T Highlights

I specified position tolerances on wheel holes for consistent stopping, flatness tolerances on tray edges for smooth sliding, and total runout on the wheel bore relative to the pin to maintain roundness under load.

Fits & Tolerances

The design used standard fit classes such as RC7 (free running) for wheels and rails, FN2 (press fit) for the pin and base, RC1 (tight clearance) for the clamp screw and frame, and LN1 (transition fit) for the clamp frame and desk.

Process Selection & Materials

Prototyping used FDM and SLA 3D printing for the clamps (engineering resin for strength, PLA for wheels), MDF for the tray and aluminum for the rails. For production, metal screws and higher‑grade wood were recommended to improve durability.

Reflection

This project demonstrated how precise tolerancing profoundly affects function. Clear labeling and orientation were critical during assembly, and the experience underscored the importance of careful measurement and planning.
