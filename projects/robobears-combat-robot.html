RoboBears Combat Robot

3 lb drum‑bot with dual‑drum weapon for 360° offense/defense.

Overview

As part of Cal’s combat robotics team, I designed, sourced and built a 3 lb battlebot with an active drum weapon. The goal was to disable opponents while minimising incoming damage and ensuring invertibility and maneuverability.

Initial Design Process

I began with a design‑matrix analysis of past combat‑robot competitions, weighing criteria like agility, stability and offensive capability. This led to choosing a drum‑bot architecture with a slanted flipper to improve engagement and bite on the opponent.

Modifications

Early prototypes featuring a single drum revealed poor defense against side impacts and belt slippage. I addressed these issues by devising a weapon system with two drum segments placed 120° apart, enabling side attacks and full 360° defense. Switching from a single to dual motor drive improved reliability at the cost of some per‑hit energy.

Final Design & Manufacturability

The chassis was laser‑cut from 6061‑T6 aluminum with pockets to optimise stiffness‑to‑weight. Waterjet‑cut A2‑steel drum sections were welded to a live shaft and constrained with nylon springs. The drive train used two direct‑drive planetary gear motors and a front ball caster for invertibility. Low‑friction tape on the caster bolt enabled self‑righting after flips.

Contributions & Takeaways

I employed SolidWorks and FEA tools to optimise the design and performed abstracted simulations of drum impacts to set load cases. I also sourced components, prepared the bill of materials and developed a detailed manufacturing plan. Through this project I gained hands‑on experience with rapid prototyping, iterative design and competitive robot combat strategy.
