ASTEME Pen Plotter Lesson

Engaging K‑8 students with a hands‑on robotics activity.

About ASTEME

ASTEME is a non‑profit learning center dedicated to inspiring K‑8 students by demonstrating how science and math are found all around us.

Roles

As a STEM instructor I designed lesson plans on a variety of topics. I adapted lessons to different age groups, sourced supplies, maintained equipment (3D printers, laser cutters, VR devices, drones, chemical closets) and engaged students in hands‑on learning.

Pen Plotter Activity

During the COVID‑19 pandemic I created a remote robotics lesson where students built a two‑joint pen‑plotting robot from popsicle‑stick arms, servo attachment pieces and clothespins. Parts were chosen so they could be easily assembled at home.

Bill of Materials & Code

I fully sourced the components and compiled a bill of materials. I programmed the pen plotter with parameterised equations for lines, shapes and letters, and wrote a conversion function using the law of cosines to translate Cartesian coordinates into servo angles.

Lesson Plan

The lesson was broken into three parts: wiring & electronics (covering electricity fundamentals, microprocessors and servo mechanics); robotic engineering (structural and materials engineering); and mathematics (demonstrating how two joints can reach any point on a plane by changing two angles).
